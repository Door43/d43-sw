===== David and Bathsheba [17-10] Daudi na Bathseba *F =====

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-17-10.jpg?nolink&}}

**One day**, when all of **David's** soldiers were away from home fighting battles, he looked out from his palace and **saw** a beautiful woman **bathing**. Her name was **Bathsheba**.

**Siku moja**, wakati askari wote wa **Daudi ** walikuwa mbali na nyumbani kwao wakipigana vita, alitazama nje kutoka kwa ikulu na **akaona** mwanamke mrembo **anaoga**. Jina lake ni **Bathseba**.
===== Important Terms (Maneno ya Muhimu): =====

  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:key-terms:david|David's (wa Daudi)]]**
  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:key-terms:bathsheba|Bathsheba (Bathseba)]]**
==== Translation Notes (Maelezo ya Tafsiri): ====

  * **One day**  - This phrase introduces an event that happened in the past, but does not state the specific time. Many languages have a similar way to begin telling a true story.
  * **siku moja**  - Huu msemo unatanguliza tukio lililofanyika siku zilizopita, lakini haielezi wakati haswa. Lugha nyingi ziko na njia sawa ya kuanza kueleza hadithi ya kweli.
  * **saw**  - Bathsheba may have been bathing at her own house, but David's palace was very high and he was able to see over walls that were lower.
  * **aliona**  - Yawezekana Bathseba alikuwa anaoga kwa nyumba yake, lakini ikulu ya Daudi ilikuwa juu sana na angeona juu ya kuta zilizokuwa chini.
  * **bathing**  - This may be translated as, "taking a bath" or, "washing herself."
  * **akioga**  - Hii inaweza kutafsiriwa kama, "kuoga" ama, "kujiosha mwenyewe."
