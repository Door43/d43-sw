===== Two Men at the Temple [34-06] Wanaume Wawili katika Hekalu =====

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-06.jpg?nolink&}}

Then **Jesus** told a **story** to some people who **trusted in their own good deeds** and **despised other people**. He said, “Two men went to the **Temple** to **pray**. One of them was a **tax collector**, and the other was a **religious leader**.”

Kisha **Yesu** akapeana **hadithi** kwa watu **walitumainia matendo yao mazuri na kupuuza watu wengine.** Akasema, "Wanaume wawili walienda kwa **hekalu** **kuomba**. Mmoja wao alikuwa **mtosha ushuru**, na yule mwingine alikuwa **kiongozi wa kindini." **

===== Translation Notes (Maelezo ya Tafsiri): =====

  * **story**  - See how you translated this term in **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:34-01|[34-01]]]**.
  * **hadithi **- Ona vile ulivyotafsiri neno hili pale **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:34-01|[34-01]]]**.
  * **trusted in their own good deeds**  - That is, "believed that their good deeds made them righteous" or, "were proud of how perfectly they kept God's law" or, "believed that their good deeds caused God to be completely pleased with them."
  * **walitumainia matendo yao mazuri **- Hiyo ni, "waliamini kuwa matendo yao mazuri iliwafanya wenye haki" ama, "walikuwa na kibuli kwa vile wanaziweka amri za Mungu" ama, "waliamini kuwa matendo yao mazuri ilifanya Mungu awe na furaha nao kabisha."
  * **despised other people**  - That is, "considered other people to be inferior to them" or, "looked down on other people."
  * **walipuuza watu wengine**  - Hiyo ni, "waliona watu wengine kama ni duni kwao" ama, "waliona watu wengine wakiwa wa kiwango ya chini.'
