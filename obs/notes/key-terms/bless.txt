====== bless bariki ======

==== (bless, blessed, blessing) (bariki, heri,baraka) ====

===== Definition: =====

To "bless" someone or something is to state a desire for positive and beneficial things to happen to them.

**"Kubariki"  mtu ama kitu ni kutamka hamu ya mambo mazuri na yenye manufaa ifanyike.**

  * To cause beneficial things to happen to someone or something.
  * **Kusababisha mambo mema yamfanyikie mtu ama yafanyike.**
  * A father would often pronounce a formal blessing on his children.
  * **Baba mara nyingi ndiye hutamka baraka rasmi juu ya wanawe.**
  * When God blesses something he causes it to be fruitful and to have a positive effect on others.
  * **Mungu akibariki kitu hukifanya kipate mazao na kuwa na athari njema kwa wengine**
The opposite of "bless" is "curse."

**Kinyume cha "bariki" ni "laani"**

  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:04-04|[04-04]]]**"I will make your name great. I will **bless**  those who **bless**  you and curse those who curse you. All families on earth will be **blessed**  because of you."
  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:04-07|[04-07]]]** Melchizedek **blessed**  Abram and said, "May God Most High who owns heaven and earth **bless**  Abram."
  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:07-03|[07-03]]]** Isaac wanted to give his **blessing**  to Esau.
  * **[[:sw:obs:notes:frames:08-05|[08-05]]]** Even in prison, Joseph remained faithful to God, and God **blessed**  him.
